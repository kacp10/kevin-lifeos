      📖 Gym Guide & Help Center

      Everything about how the Gym module thinks. The app stays clean — all the explanations live here.

      🏋️ How to log a workout

      Open Gym. The app shows today's workout automatically based on the day of the week. Each exercise is a card with its photo, target muscles, sets × reps and rest time.

      Every planned set is shown as a row. The active row has two boxes: weight (kg) and reps. Type them and tap ✓ — the set is saved and the next one becomes active. When you finish all sets the exercise turns green (✓ done). You can add an Extra set, or undo any set with ✕.

      Training a different muscle group today? Use the dropdown at the top to pick another day's plan. Sets are always saved to today's date.

      🧠 Smart history & targets

      Each exercise remembers your last session and shows it (e.g. 20kg×10, 20kg×10, 22.5kg×8). Based on it, you get a target for today:

      • If you hit the top of the rep range on every set last time → it suggests +2.5 kg.
      • If not → it suggests the same weight, +1 rep.

      The weight/reps boxes come pre-filled with that suggestion so logging takes seconds. This is the core of real progress: progressive overload — doing a little more over time.

      📈 How progression works

      After you log sets, each exercise shows a badge comparing today with your last session:

      • ⬆ Stronger — more weight or more total volume (weight × reps).
      • ➡ Matched — same performance. Still a win.
      • ⬇ Below — a bit less. Normal on bad-sleep or low-food days. The trend over weeks is what counts.

      ⚖️ How the Goal Weight recommendation is calculated

      If you leave Goal Weight blank, the app recommends one from your height and physical goal, using BMI as a sensible anchor:

      • Goal about fat loss / abs / definition → BMI ≈ 22 (lean, abs-visible look).
      • Goal about muscle / mass / strength → BMI ≈ 24.5.
      • Anything else (recomp/athletic) → BMI ≈ 23.

      Formula: goal kg = target BMI × (height in m)². If you're already leaner than the fat-loss target, it eases the goal toward maintaining. It updates on its own as your goal, height or weight change — it's never a fixed-forever number. You can always set your own value in Edit, and it will respect it.

      BMI is a rough guide, not a body-fat measure. With muscle, the real proof is the mirror, the waist tape and how your clothes fit — not the exact number.

      📐 How to register measurements

      Tap + Log this week. Use a simple sewing tape, in the morning, before eating, relaxed (don't suck in), same day each week. Leave blank what you didn't measure.

      • Waist — around the navel. The #1 fat-loss signal.
      • Chest — across the nipples, arms down.
      • Arm — flexed bicep, thickest point.
      • Hips — widest part of the glutes.
      • Thigh — highest point, under the glute.
      • Weight — same scale, morning, after bathroom.

      📉 How to read the charts

      The progress chart plots weight (gold) and waist (purple) over the weeks. Both trending down = you're losing fat. The measurement cards show your latest value vs your start, with a green arrow when waist/weight drop.

      ❓ FAQ

      Will lifting make me bulky instead of losing fat? No. Muscle and fat are different tissues. Fat loss comes from a calorie deficit (food); lifting keeps the muscle that gives you shape. Without lifting you'd just get "skinny-fat".

      When will I see changes? 3–4 weeks to notice yourself, 6–8 weeks for others. One week shows nothing.

      The scale went up this week. Water, food and a new training stimulus move it daily. Judge by the 2-week trend, not one reading.

      Do I need my weight to start? No. Log what you can; add weight when you have a scale.

      Is my data safe / does it cost anything? Everything is free and stored in your own database. No premium, no external service required.
